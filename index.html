What Makes Us Different

Swipe for more

Local Founder & Teacher

Founded locally by an educator with 12 years of teaching experience.

Knowing about tech is one thing, but knowing how to teach it is another! We skip the heavy jargon and focus on making complex concepts fun, safe, and easy for kids to understand.

Smart & Secure Learning

By utilizing familiar mobile devices, our program capitalizes on how kids already interact with tech to maximize learning.

Instruction is delivered using program-provided smartphones running a dynamic, gamified quiz platform for real-time engagement.

Unmatched Safety

Students interact with a customized Google Gemini model pre-installed on our devices.

This allows us to strictly filter content, ensuring students won't access anything inappropriate while they explore.

Critical AI Literacy

We go beyond just showing kids how to use AI. We teach them how to truly understand it.

Students are empowered to spot algorithmic bias, protect their data privacy, and consider the real-world ethical impacts of the technology they use every day.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common inquiries from parents.

What age group is this camp for?

BrAIn Camp is designed for students ages 8 to 11. However, if you believe your child would be a great fit (such as a sibling, or a deeply curious and creative student just outside this range), please email us at brAInCampWA@gmail.com to discuss!

What are the daily hours?

Our full-day sessions run Monday through Friday, from 9:00 AM to 4:00 PM.

Does my child need to bring a phone or laptop?

No! Instruction is delivered using secure, program-provided smartphones. All necessary hardware and technology is included in the tuition fee.

What should my camper bring?

Please pack a daily snack, a water bottle, and a lunch for your child. All tech tools and camp materials are provided by the program.

How do you ensure screen safety?

Safety is our priority. Our provided devices interact with a customized AI model and enforce strict behavioral and content filters to ensure students won't access anything inappropriate.

Will this encourage my child to use AI to complete their school assignments?

We take academic integrity seriously. Our curriculum teaches AI as a "brainstorming partner" or "learning assistant," not a replacement for unique thought. We emphasize that over-reliance on AI can hinder their own voice and critical thinking skills.

How does the camp address AI's impact on artists and copyright?

We center the rights of creators. During our Image Forge day, we critically examine how AI models are trained and discuss the importance of consent, copyright, and fair compensation for human artists.

Does the program cover the environmental impact of AI?

Yes. Running AI requires significant energy and water. We discuss this ecological cost, teaching students about the carbon footprint of digital tools and the need for climate-conscious technology.

How do you handle algorithmic bias and other ethical challenges?

We equip kids with the digital literacy to spot algorithmic bias and understand data privacy. We aren't teaching kids to blindly consume AI, but to ask important questions about how these technologies affect society.

Do you offer scholarships or financial aid?

We believe every child should have the opportunity to develop digital literacy. If you are struggling financially, email us at brAInCampWA@gmail.com to discuss scholarship options.

Who do I contact if I have more questions?

You can reach out to us anytime at brAInCampWA@gmail.com. We are happy to help!
